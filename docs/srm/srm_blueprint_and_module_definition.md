# SRM Blueprint and Module Definition

## 1. Product Vision
Build a supplier relationship management platform for a logistics and supply chain business that manages the full lifecycle of transport and logistics providers — from onboarding and qualification to compliance, performance, relationship management, risk control, and commercial governance.

The module should feel comparable in depth to enterprise SRM products such as SAP Ariba, while being adapted to logistics realities like:
- carriers
- airlines
- shipping lines
- truckers
- warehouses
- couriers
- customs brokers
- agents
- specialist subcontractors

The goal is not only to store supplier records. The goal is to create one governed operating layer for:
- supplier master data
- qualification
- documents
- compliance
- performance
- communications
- scorecards
- future sourcing/procurement linkage

## 2. In-Scope Supplier Types
- Ocean carriers and NVOCCs
- Airlines and air consolidators
- Rail operators and intermodal providers
- Truckers, hauliers, and last-mile carriers
- Courier and parcel providers
- Warehouse operators and value-added logistics providers
- Customs brokers and clearance providers
- Agents, overseas partners, and destination service providers
- Technology or service vendors related to logistics execution where relevant

## 3. Core Functional Domains
### Supplier Master
Single supplier record with hierarchy, categories, geography, contacts, and service scope.

### Supplier Onboarding
Capture applications, required documents, approvals, and activation workflow.

### Qualification & Segmentation
Approve supplier for lanes, modes, service types, or strategic categories.

### Compliance & Document Control
Manage insurance, licenses, certificates, contracts, sanctions and expiry tracking.

### Performance Management
Scorecards, KPI trends, incident counts, responsiveness, claims, and quality ratings.

### Risk Management
Financial, operational, compliance, capacity, concentration, and geopolitical risk.

### Relationship Management
Reviews, notes, action plans, meetings, ownership, and strategic development.

### Search / Reporting
Analytics, segmentation, supplier directory, scorecards, auditability.

### Procurement/Sourcing Linkage
Future tie-in to tenders, tariffs, contract sourcing, and invoice audit.

## 4. Design Principles
- One supplier record, multiple service categories and geographies.
- Strong document and compliance control with expiry logic.
- Role-based approval and audit trail for onboarding and changes.
- Performance and risk should be measurable and explainable.
- Support both transactional providers and strategic partners.
- Ready to connect later to tendering, tariff management, contract management, control tower, and invoice auditing.
